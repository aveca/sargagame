The context

A regional problem, a local execution.

Since 2018, mass sargassum landings have been managed under national response plans. Large public budgets have been committed to collection and treatment, and the effort keeps being extended. But on the ground, it's the towns, the local authorities and their contractors who trigger the cleanup — beach after beach, day after day.

Sargassum gives no warning: a landing can hit one beach and spare another a few kilometers away. Without anticipation, crews go where the algae is already visible — that is, too late, once decomposition has started and the smell (H2S) is already bothering residents. A per-beach forecast, backed by a dated record, turns that reaction into control: you decide where to position equipment before the algae touches the sand — you know the end of the story before the smell.

What's at stake

Public money committed — and an expectation of measurable results.

Public sargassum funding isn't judged by the amount spent, but by how effective every collected ton is and by the ability to prove it. Decide where to deploy tomorrow, and document what was done.

Frequently asked

What local governments ask us.

Does the tool replace the national plan or the official bulletin?

No. It backs them: the public plan sets the framework and the resources, the official bulletin gives the regional risk. Our tool translates that into a per-beach forecast and a dated record — to decide where to intervene and document the effort.

Is the data usable for our reports and our funders?

Yes. The record is dated and auditable per beach (export available), which documents the cleanup effort and supports transparency with both residents and the plan's funders.

What is the forecast's real reliability?

We publish it on the reliability page, dated and per beach. Our "clean water" forecasts verify at a rate we display alongside its sample size. Alerts are issued cautiously until the satellite confirms them — and we publish that limit too.

Can we share it with our cleanup contractor?

Yes. The per-beach landing forecast feeds the scheduling of your contractor's crews and equipment, over 7 days.

Which beaches and regions are covered?

All tracked beaches in Martinique and Guadeloupe, with a dated record per beach — the French Antilles, the only coast where our accuracy is validated and published, with the network rolling out across the Caribbean.
