Where the money goes when you react

Three leaks, all triggered by the same information lag.

When you discover the landing the same morning, every response costs its "emergency" version. The overspend isn't the event — it's having learned about it too late.

🧹

Emergency, over-staffed cleanups

Contractor called at the last minute, crews on overtime, equipment rented at peak price. For reference, collection already runs around €134 per ton in normal conditions — the rush only inflates the bill.

📅

Cancellations and refunds

On affected stretches at peak season, bookings can drop 30 to 40% (a regional market estimate, not a measure of your property). A guest who sees the seaweed in a photo cancels; a guest already on-site asks for a goodwill gesture. Every empty or discounted room is margin that won't come back this season.

⭐

Reputation, which costs long after

A review mentioning the smell or a closed beach drags on your property's score well beyond the stay in question. Reputation degrades fast and rebuilds slowly — it's the most expensive leak because it's the most durable.

What anticipation changes

The same event, managed instead of endured.

Anticipating doesn't make sargassum disappear. It turns a morning crisis into a decision made a few days earlier — when the cleanup can be scheduled and the messaging prepared calmly.

Season endured

  • You discover the landing when you open the shutters
  • Contractor called on emergency, inflated rate
  • Disappointed guests on-site, improvised goodwill gestures
  • Negative reviews you can no longer catch up on
  • No record to show the effort you made

Season anticipated

  • The mat is spotted offshore several days ahead
  • Collection scheduled at the right slot, the right price
  • Guests told in advance, alternatives offered
  • A clean waterfront: fewer reviews about the smell
  • A dated history to reassure and document

Concretely: a per-beach 7-day forecast lets you book collection resources before prices spike, shift or rearrange sensitive arrivals, and send guests an honest message — "here's the real state, here's what we're doing." Reassuring upfront costs once; refunding afterward costs every time.
