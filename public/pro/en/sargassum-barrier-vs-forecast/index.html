The right debate

The question isn't "barrier versus forecast".

When a sargassum season looms, people look for a single fix: install a floating barrier, or plan the cleanup better. That's fair — but pitting the physical barrier against satellite forecasting is the wrong debate. These are two tools of different natures: one acts on the sargassum that's already there, the other on the decision to handle it at the right moment.

A barrier doesn't erase uncertainty: you still need to know when the mats will hit, so you can mobilize the pumping and collection crews behind it. A forecast, on its own, collects nothing. The right strategy isn't to choose — it's to put forecasting upstream of your physical means.

The comparison

Three approaches, three complementary roles.

Not a single row below runs the barrier down: it stays useful on an exposed site. But read column by column, the complementarity is obvious.

The leverage effect

Why forecasting multiplies the resources you already have.

Your barriers, your equipment and your crews are scarce resources. Forecasting doesn't replace them — it decides where and when to concentrate them, which changes their whole return.

🎯

The right effort, in the right place

Instead of spreading resources blind, you concentrate boom, pumping and collection on the beaches that will be hit in the coming days.

⏱️

Before the landing, not after

Emptying a boom and mobilizing a crew costs less anticipated than in a weekend emergency. Forecasting shifts the intervention upstream of the crisis.

🧾

Frequently asked

Barrier, forecast: what people ask us.

Do I have to choose between a barrier and forecasting?

No. They're two complementary tools: the barrier acts on the sargassum that's already there, the forecast on the decision to handle it at the right moment. On a highly exposed site, the combination is the optimum — the forecast tells you when to reinforce and empty the boom.

Does forecasting replace the cleanup?

No, and that's the key point: a forecast collects nothing. It makes your cleanup and your barriers pay off by telling you where and when to concentrate resources, up to 7 days ahead. It's a multiplier for your existing resources.

Can you guarantee a floating barrier's effectiveness?

No, and we don't claim to: a barrier's effectiveness depends on the site, the swell and its upkeep. The only thing we put a number on is our own forecasting performance — published live on this page and detailed on our reliability page.

What's the real reliability of your forecast?

It's published, dated and per beach on our reliability page. Our "clean sea" forecasts verify at a high rate (~75-78% across all conditions) that we display live with the sample size — never a frozen figure. Alerts are issued cautiously until the satellite confirms them — and we publish that limit too. Reliability is validated in the French Antilles and rolling out across the Caribbean; we don't claim validated accuracy on coasts we haven't measured yet.
